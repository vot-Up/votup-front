# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Votup serves administrators, candidates, and voters in a voting system. Administrators manage users, candidates, voters, plates, and election settings. Candidates need a clear view of their candidacy and plate participation. Voters need a fast, trustworthy flow to authenticate, understand their options, and cast a vote without confusion.

## Product Purpose

Votup exists to make digital election management and voting clear, reliable, and accessible. Success means election operators can manage the process with confidence, candidates can understand their role, and voters can complete the voting flow on desktop or mobile with minimal friction.

## Brand Personality

Institutional, trustworthy, direct, and warm. The product should feel serious enough for election workflows, but not cold or bureaucratic. The interface should reduce anxiety, make status obvious, and keep actions understandable.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, excessive card grids, cold gray admin themes, decorative effects that do not support a task, vague productivity copy, and playful visuals that weaken trust in the voting process.

## Design Principles

- Trust before decoration: visual choices must make the election workflow feel secure, legible, and accountable.
- Mobile voting is a first-class path: voter-facing flows must be easy to complete on small touch screens.
- Admin density should stay calm: management screens can be information-rich, but hierarchy and spacing must keep scanning effortless.
- Use warmth without noise: the cream canvas and saturated accents should humanize the product while preserving operational clarity.
- One vocabulary per action: the same concepts, controls, and states should look and read consistently across admin, candidate, and voter surfaces.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Preserve strong contrast, visible focus indicators, keyboard navigation, reduced-motion compatibility, and minimum 44px touch targets for interactive controls. Avoid relying on color alone for status, selection, or error communication.
